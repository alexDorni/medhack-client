Measles

Measles is an airborne disease caused by a virus

Symptoms may develop about eight to 12 days after you’re exposed. Symptoms can last 10 to 14 days.

Measles is also called rubeola, 10-day measles or red measles. It’s not the same as German measles, or rubella.

The most common symptoms of measles include:

  • A high fever.
  • Tiredness.
  • A barky cough.
  • Red or bloodshot eyes.

The side effects that do occur are usually mild and may include:

  • Soreness, redness, or swelling where the shot was given.
  • Fever.
  • Mild rash.
  • Temporary pain and stiffness in the joints.
